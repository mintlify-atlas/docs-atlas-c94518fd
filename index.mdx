AI Protocol Kit is a set of practical behavioral contracts you load into an AI context before it starts working. Each protocol tells the AI how to approach a task, what to verify, when to stop, and what kind of output is acceptable — replacing vague prompts with explicit operating rules.

These are not magic prompts. They are working contracts for real, messy situations where the AI should not simply improvise.

## What this kit prevents

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Premature convergence" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    AI rushing to the obvious answer before exploring the problem space.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Invented facts" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    AI presenting inferred or hallucinated claims as verified findings.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Wrong reader fit" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    Writing for the wrong audience or tone without closing the reader model first.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Repo publication errors" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    Committing, pushing, or publishing before workspace and risk are audited.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Agentic drift" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    Multi-step AI sessions expanding scope or approving their own work without human checkpoints.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Flat compromises" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    Constraint conflicts resolved by splitting the difference rather than surfacing the dominant force.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  **Default rule: use one protocol.** Stack only when one protocol governs the session and another governs the artifact or publication target. See the [Stacking Guide](/stacking-guide) for justified combinations.
</Note>
